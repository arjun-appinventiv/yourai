1. End-to-End Message Pipeline

Every message flows through 5 stages. The user never sees this — it all happens in under 2 seconds.

💬
User Message
Text, document upload, or follow-up question
🎯
Intent Classifier
Picks the right operation (confidence ≥ 0.75)
👥
Persona Layer
Applies role-specific tone, format & prompt modifier
🗃
Source Resolution
User doc → Knowledge Pack → Global KB → Fallback
⚡
Streamed Response
With source badge showing where the answer came from
⚠️ Low Confidence Branch
If the classifier scores below 0.75, Alex doesn't guess. Instead, it asks: "Are you asking about your uploaded contract, or a general legal question?" — then routes based on the user's answer.
❌ No Answer Found
All sources exhausted (user doc, knowledge pack, global KB). Alex displays the fallback message: "I couldn't find a clear answer in your documents or the knowledge base. Could you clarify?"

4. How the Final Prompt is Assembled

Example: A Paralegal uploads a contract. The intent classifier activates Contract Review. Here's how the prompt is built:

🎯 Operation Prompt
"You are Alex, a contract analysis specialist. You identify risks, flag non-standard clauses, and compare terms against the firm's approved playbook. Always cite the clause number and page. Output a risk summary at the end of every analysis."
+
👥 Persona Modifier
"Use clear, accessible language. Provide checklists and actionable steps. Explain legal terms when first used."
(Appended because the user selected "Paralegal / Legal Assistant" during onboarding)
+
🎤 Tone Override
Conversational (overrides Contract Review's default "Formal" tone)
The paralegal gets friendlier language even when reviewing contracts.
+
📋 Merged Format Rules
Always cite source Bullet lists Risk summary
"Cite source" and "Bullet lists" from the Paralegal persona + "Risk summary" from Contract Review operation = merged set.
=
⚡ Final Prompt
A contract analysis specialist who uses conversational tone, explains legal terms, provides checklists, cites every clause with page number, and ends with a risk summary.

6. Same Question, Different Persona

All 4 users ask: "What are the termination clauses in this contract?" with the same contract attached. Same operation (Contract Review). Different response format.

💼
Partner Response Formal
Executive Summary: The agreement contains 3 termination provisions. Two carry moderate risk.

Risk Assessment:
🔴 High: Clause 14.2 — Termination for convenience with only 15-day notice (industry standard: 30 days)
🟠 Medium: Clause 14.3 — No cure period for material breach
🟢 Low: Clause 14.1 — Standard expiration clause

Recommended Action: Negotiate 14.2 to 30-day notice and add 15-day cure period to 14.3.
⚖️
Associate Response Formal
Termination Clauses Analysis:

1. Clause 14.1 (p. 23) — Expiration on the stated end date. Standard provision, no issues.

2. Clause 14.2 (p. 23-24) — Either party may terminate for convenience with 15 days' written notice. Per UCC §2-309(3), reasonable notification is required. 15 days may be insufficient for complex commercial relationships. See Leibel v. Raynor (1991).

3. Clause 14.3 (p. 24) — Termination for material breach, effective immediately upon notice. No cure period provided. Compare with ABA Model Contract §8.2 which recommends 30-day cure periods.

Next Step: Draft a redline proposing 30-day notice for 14.2 and adding a 15-day cure period to 14.3.
✅
Paralegal Response Conversational
I found 3 termination clauses in the contract. Here's a quick breakdown:

✓ Clause 14.1 (page 23) — The contract ends on the date listed. Nothing unusual here.
⚠️ Clause 14.2 (pages 23-24) — Either side can cancel with just 15 days' notice. That's shorter than the usual 30 days. ("Termination for convenience" means they don't need a reason.)
⚠️ Clause 14.3 (page 24) — If one side breaks the contract, the other can end it immediately with no chance to fix the problem. ("Cure period" means time to correct a breach — this contract doesn't include one.)

Source: Uploaded contract, pages 23-24.
💻
Legal Ops Response Concise
3 termination clauses found:
• 14.1 — Expiration (standard)
• 14.2 — Convenience termination, 15-day notice
• 14.3 — Material breach, immediate, no cure period

Next: Flag 14.2 and 14.3 for attorney review. Tag in matter management system.

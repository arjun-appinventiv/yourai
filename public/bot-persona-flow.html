Alex Answers 3 Questions Before Every Response

Every time a user sends a message, Alex silently runs through these 3 questions in under 2 seconds. The user just sees the final answer.

🎯

WHAT is being asked?

Is this a contract review? Legal research? Drafting request? General chat?

→ Picks an Operation
👤

WHO is asking?

Is this a Partner who wants the bottom line? Or a Paralegal who needs terms explained?

→ Applies Persona Format
📎

WHERE is the answer?

Is it in the uploaded document? The knowledge base? Or is there no answer?

→ Searches Sources in Order

Step-by-Step: What Happens When a User Sends a Message

Let's say a Paralegal uploads a contract and asks: "What are the termination clauses?"

1

User Sends Message

The Paralegal uploads AcmeCorp_ServiceAgreement.pdf and types: "What are the termination clauses in this contract?"
2

Intent Classifier Picks Operation

The AI reads the message and detects: user uploaded a contract + asking about clauses. It picks Contract Review mode (confidence: 0.94).
Contract Review prompt loads: "You are Alex, a contract analysis specialist. Identify risks, flag non-standard clauses, cite clause numbers and pages..."
3

Persona Format Applied

The AI checks: this user selected "Paralegal / Legal Assistant" during onboarding. It layers on the persona settings.
Added to prompt: "Use clear, accessible language. Explain legal terms when first used."
Tone changed: Formal → Conversational
Format rules: Citations + Bullet Lists (from Paralegal) + Risk Summary (from Contract Review)
4

Source Resolution

The AI searches for the answer. It finds termination clauses on pages 23-24 of the uploaded contract. Search stops at Step 1 — no need to check KB.
✓ User's document → FOUND (pages 23-24)
— Knowledge Pack → skipped
— Global KB → skipped
— Fallback → skipped
5

Response Streamed to User

Alex generates a conversational, bullet-pointed contract review with legal terms explained — and a blue source badge.
💬 "I found 3 termination clauses in the contract..."
🔴 Clause 14.2 — 15-day notice (shorter than standard)...
🔵 Answered from: your document

Same Question, 4 Different Answers

All 4 users upload the same contract and ask the same question: "What are the termination clauses in this contract?"
Same operation (Contract Review). Same document. Completely different response.

💼

Partner Gets This

Formal
Executive Summary: 3 termination provisions. Two carry moderate risk.

🔴 HIGH — Clause 14.2 (p. 23): Termination for convenience with only 15-day notice. Standard is 30 days.

🟠 MEDIUM — Clause 14.3 (p. 24): No cure period for material breach.

🟢 LOW — Clause 14.1 (p. 23): Standard expiration. No concern.

Recommended Action: Negotiate 14.2 to 30 days. Add 15-day cure period to 14.3.
🔵 Answered from: your document
⚖️

Associate Gets This

Formal
1. Clause 14.1 (p. 23) — Expiration on stated date. Standard, no issues.

2. Clause 14.2 (p. 23-24) — Termination for convenience, 15 days' notice. Per UCC §2-309(3), "reasonable notification" is required. Courts held 15 days insufficient for complex relationships. See Leibel v. Raynor (1991).

3. Clause 14.3 (p. 24) — Material breach, immediate termination, no cure period. ABA Model Contract §8.2 recommends 30-day cure periods.

Next Step: Draft redline: 30-day notice for 14.2, 15-day cure for 14.3. Cite Leibel v. Raynor.
🔵 Answered from: your document
✅

Paralegal Gets This

Conversational
I found 3 termination clauses. Here's a quick breakdown:

✓ Clause 14.1 (page 23) — The contract ends on the date listed. Nothing unusual.

⚠️ Clause 14.2 (pages 23-24) — Either side can cancel with just 15 days' notice. That's shorter than the usual 30 days. ("Termination for convenience" means they don't need a reason.)

⚠️ Clause 14.3 (page 24) — If one side breaks the contract, the other can end it immediately — no chance to fix it first. ("Cure period" means time to correct a mistake. This contract doesn't include one.)

Source: Uploaded contract, pages 23-24.
🔵 Answered from: your document
💻

Legal Ops Gets This

Concise
3 termination clauses found:

• 14.1 — Expiration (standard, no risk)
• 14.2 — Convenience, 15-day notice (flag: below 30-day standard)
• 14.3 — Material breach, immediate, no cure period

Next: Flag 14.2 and 14.3 for attorney review. Tag in matter management system.
🔵 Answered from: your document

How the Final Prompt is Built

The persona doesn't replace the operation — it layers on top. Here's how it works when a Paralegal triggers Contract Review:

🎯 Operation Prompt
"You are Alex, a contract analysis specialist. Identify risks, flag non-standard clauses, compare against the firm's playbook. Cite clause number and page."
+
👤 Persona Modifier
"Use clear, accessible language. Provide checklists and actionable steps. Explain legal terms when first used."
↑ Because the user selected "Paralegal" during onboarding
+
🎤 Tone Override
Conversational ← overrides Contract Review's default "Formal"
The paralegal gets friendlier language even in contract reviews.
+
📋 Merged Rules
Citations Bullet Lists Risk Summary
Citations & Bullets from Paralegal + Risk Summary from Contract Review = merged set
=
⚡ Final Alex
A contract specialist who speaks in plain English, explains legal jargon, uses bullet points, cites every clause with page number, and includes risk scores.
